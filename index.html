How I built Cars-World
July 2025 ¬∑ React Native
Read

Creating CarsWorld, my first mobile app, was a thrilling but demanding trip.My love of automobiles and my fascination with mobile app development inspired the concept.I wanted to design a system whereby customers could peruse various car models, examine in-depth specs, and conveniently book or buy them.

I selected React Native with Expo SDK 53 so that I could quickly develop and test on both Android and iOS.Using a global Theme Context, I included car categories, detailed pages with price and year, an "Add to Cart" button, a booking form with validation, and even a dark mode toggle.

Navigation was among the most important things I learned. I used React Navigation to make a smooth flow between screens, which included a bottom tab bar for quick access to the Category, Search, Cart, and Profile pages.I also discovered how to show order history in the profile page and how to preserve it.

There were difficulties along the way. I had trouble with form validation, styling, and state management, but every challenge taught me something fresh.I had not just a working app by the end but also a far better grasp of how mobile apps are organized.
